Maine

Maine is special for various reasons – for example, it is the only state in the US whose name consists of only one syllabus! Wild blueberries can also be found exclusively in this state. There are a lot of nice places to visit in Maine. From our own experience, we can particularly recommend Portland, Camden, and of course Acadia National Park.

Portland

Portland can be reached from Boston within a bit more than 1.5 hours by car or by train (Amtrak from North Station). It offers many nice restaurants (for example, a floating restaurant that used to be a car ferry), shopping opportunities and beautiful views over the sea.

Camden/Rockport, ME

Camden is a beautiful small, coastal city. It offers nice restaurants right at Camden harbor, from where also different boat and sailing tours depart. See, e.g., Camden-harbor-cruises (we took the Eco-Tour, which we can highly recommend!) or maineschooners . Nearby Rockport also has nice restaurants and several accommodation options.

If you are in the area and like hiking, we strongly recommend the Ragged Mountain trail. Parts of it are quite steep and strenuous, but in the end, one is rewarded with an amazing view over the coastline.

For more information, see also here (we started the hike from the Route 17 Trailhead (Georges Highland Path parking lot)

View from Ragged Mountain trail

Acadia National Park

If you have some time to travel in the area, Acadia National Park should be on your list. This national park combines sea, small mountains, and forests all together and offers a great variety of hikes as well as amazing views. For more information visit nps.gov/acadia or acadiamagic.com
